BIODIVERSITY

Video Courtesy: The Royal Society

Biodiversity is the variety of life on Earth, including genetic differences, species diversity, and ecosystems. It supports vital services like clean air, water, food, and climate regulation. For example, forests absorb over 2.6 billion tonnes of carbon dioxide annually, helping fight climate change (WHO, 2025). However, biodiversity is rapidly declining, with one million species at risk, threatening these essential services and increasing health risks. Though scientists have made progress in understanding biodiversity, much remains unknown, and human actions are accelerating its loss. Urgent action is needed to protect ecosystems and preserve life on Earth (Purvis & Hector, 2000).

Scientific Innovation

Start Park

A project in Italy that creates green, sustainable urban spaces. It uses plants and natural methods to clean soil and save rainwater, helping cities cope with climate change(Climate Action, 2021).

Living Shorelines

A way to fight coastal erosion by building artificial reefs with oyster shells instead of using expensive seawalls. This is a natural and cheaper solution(Climate Action, 2021).

Frozen Ark

A project that stores the DNA of endangered animals in liquid nitrogen. This helps protect their gentic information in case the species goes extinct(Climate Action, 2021).

The 2010 International Year of Biodiversity

Image Courtesy: Biodiversity Concervation Center

The 2010 International Year of Biodiversity, declared by the United Nations, aimed to raise awareness about the importance of biodiversity and encourage actions to protect it. It highlighted the essential role biodiversity plays in human life, affecting food, health, and well-being, and emphasized the connection between humans and ecosystems (Time and Date, 2010). The year featured educational campaigns, conferences, and global events, promoting the inclusion of biodiversity in development plans and encouraging responsible resource use through initiatives like the Green Wave campaign (UNESCO, 2010)
