BIOTECHNOLOGY

Video courtesy: Jairenz Batu

Biotechnology is a scientific field that utilizes living organisms, to develop technologies and products intended to improve human life (AL-Eitan & Alnemri, 2022). Often referred to as Biotech, it has existed since the beginning of civilization with the domestication of plants, animals, and the discovery of fermentation (Lewis & Barney, 2019). Biotechnology combines biology with engineering and technology to create solutions in medicine, agriculture, industry, and environmental conservation. It plays a crucial role in addressing global challenges such as disease, hunger, pollution, and sustainable energy.

Scientific Principles

Genetic Engineering

To manipulate and modify the genetic material of an organism to incorporate desirable traits. The main pillar of genetic engineering is recombinant DNA technology, which is a technique to alter the genes of an organism by inserting the desired gene into a host (Byjus, 2017).

Bioprocess Engineering

Focuses on designing and optimizing biological and mechanical processes required to create various products within life sciences (KGI, 2020).

GENETIC MODIFICATION

Image courtesy: newsbytesapp

Genetically Modified Organisms, are organisms whose genome has been engineered in the laboratory in order to favour the expression of desired physiological traits or the generation of desired biological products (Diaz & Fridovich-Keil, 2018).

Image courtesy: iStock

Process of Genetic Modification

Genetic modification involves scientific methods that may include DNA extraction and gene isolation, rDNA construction, and gene insertion. It uses recombinant DNA technology and reproductive cloning. In reproductive cloning, a nucleus is extracted from a cell of the individual to be cloned and is inserted into the enucleated cytoplasm of a host egg.

The process results in the generation of an offspring that is genetically identical to the donor individual. Recombinant DNA technology, on the other hand, involves the insertion of one or more individual genes from an organism of one species into the DNA of another. Which results in the organism possessing the desired genetic makeup (Diaz & Fridovich-Keil, 2018).

GENETICALLY MODIFIED ORGANISMS (GMO)

Image courtesy: iStock

Genetic Modification is the process of altering the genetic makeup of an organism. This has been done indirectly for thousands of years by controlled, or selective, breeding of plants and animals. Modern biotechnology has made it easier and faster to target a specific gene for more precise alteration of the organism through genetic engineering (Ross, 2019).

Benefits of GMOs

Agricultural

GMOs are engineered for higher productivity, thus increasing crop yields and helping to address food security. They also enhance nutritional value, such as the biofortified GMOs like Golden Rice, which combat malnutrition.

Environmental

Pest-resistant GMOs decrease reliance on synthetic pesticides, lowering soil and water pollution. Drought-tolerant and nitrogen-efficient crops reduce water and fertilizer use.

Medical & Pharmaceutical

GMOs help produce pharmaceuticals and vaccines, develop diagnostic tests, and enable gene therapy for treating genetic diseases. They also allow researchers to study the functions of genes in human and animal diseases.

Economic

GMOs help increase farm incomes, reduce pesticide use, and potentially lower food prices.
